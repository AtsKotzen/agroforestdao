History

We are a community of people who believe that it is possible to manage perennial production areas with a hybrid model between local and global. Using blockchain technology we are building gamified engagement models in governance, education, training, reputation building and retirement.

Our proposal is a forestry adaptation of the CSA, where the community is global, and production is intergenerational.


Modern society was built on a model of market agriculture, which exploits the producer and the planet. Because of this, since 1950 the world has been experiencing a process of rural exodus, as market agriculture simply does not bring advantages to the small producer. But how can we conceive of community agriculture in the 21st century?

Before colonization there was community farming among indigenous tribes in Brazil. There was collective building of knowledge, training, counseling and caring for the elders. Forests of food were inherited from previous generations and it was tacit that more food should be planted for future generations. Anyone who dedicated himself to his community could know that as he grew older he would also be taken care of. This gave peace of mind and tranquility for people to engage in activities that felt good without charge but with responsibility.

The need to sell the final product does not value the process. People do not choose activities that they identify with, but those that bring in the most money. Many farmers are not even interested in eating what they produce, just selling and competing at the fair.

However, currently there is a different movement. After the era of “organic” labels, viable only for those close to cities, the CSA movement (community that sustains agriculture) emerged, which proposed paying the farmer in advance, so that he could dedicate himself to his service and not have to worry about sales and distribution. This model is present in several cities, generally focused on vegetables, with no ambition to include wood or grain crops.


Thanks to the immutability and transparency of the blockchain, we can create links between co-producers. We can create frameworks for education and training where we recognize apprentices, mentors and masters, and thus create a “career plan” for urban or rural youth who simply want to have dignity and make a positive difference in their community.

Simple credentials like POAPs can serve to allow access to more specific areas of the community, and, along with other NFT credentials, can be used to build reputation and encourage engagement. Incentives can be product discounts, experience vouchers, course vouchers, tourism vouchers, royalties in a marketplace or retroactive funds proportional to the member's contribution (retirement). High reputation = more chance of receiving support from the community.
The construction of this socio-economic ecosystem depends on the natural ecosystem and therefore we need to focus on collaborating with collective production and organizing fair distribution. After a few laps in the sun we will have learned valuable lessons, and if we manage to keep up with the force of nature, our seeds will have increased exponentially. There is an initial investment, a risk shared with the farmer, but a few months after the planting is growing vigorously, the fear goes away, and with the first harvests it is seen that the choice to invest was right.

We then launched the possibility of investing in Feijão Amigo!

A collection of 40 NFTs. Each represents 5% of the estimated cost of 4 months of cultivation and distributing 5% of the harvest (however, we were told that we should not keep these percentages because of the CVM…).
Buyers of that NFT will be on a list of initial supporters, who will receive future benefits.

We will create a category for pre-planting supporters, the most difficult moment was when we didn't have beautiful photos to show anyone.

But now we have pictures!

When creating the NFT we defined a split in 0xsplit.xyz, directing to 4 multisigs percentages for each fund:
- 3% for apprenticeship;
- 3% for mentorship;
- 3% for content creation;
- 3% for retirement.

Our initial idea is that you need to create solid social contracts first and then create a token. We are running for the ReFiDAO and Commonstack award, which deals precisely with tokenomics.

In addition to some sites that already know about the proposal, we are partners in a web2.5 network of more than 600 registered projects called Ekonavi. A source of experience vouchers. The tokenomics of these vouchers is a subject we can delve into if you like, perhaps locking up part of the investments (and vouchers). For governance power perhaps we could consider not only the amount of tokens but a link metric of digital and IRL engagement.
